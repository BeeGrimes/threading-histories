Community Outreach & Collaboration

Building connections, sharing knowledge, and growing together

Our Commitment to Community

Threading Histories is more than an academic projectâ€”it's a living archive that belongs to the communities whose histories it represents. We recognize that the knowledge systems of Nigerian women's textile traditions are not static historical artifacts but continue to thrive through contemporary artisans, cultural practitioners, and descendant communities.

This project was built with the understanding that colonial archives, while valuable, represent only partial perspectives. We actively seek to center the voices, knowledge, and corrections of those most connected to these traditions.

Community Stakeholders

We welcome engagement from diverse community members, including:

Nigerian Diaspora Communities

Individuals with family histories, oral traditions, and cultural knowledge of textile practices.

Contemporary Artisans

Textile practitioners continuing traditional techniques and innovating new forms.

Cultural Heritage Organizations

Museums, cultural centers, and preservation initiatives in Nigeria and internationally.

Scholars & Researchers

Historians, anthropologists, and researchers with specialized knowledge or archival materials.

How to Contribute

Submission Guidelines

We welcome the following types of contributions:

  • Oral Histories: Family stories, memories, and personal accounts of textile traditions
  • Corrections: Updates to location data, craft techniques, or historical narratives
  • Additional Sources: Photographs, documents, or archival materials not currently included
  • Contemporary Connections: Information about living traditions and contemporary artisans
  • Translation Assistance: Help with Yoruba, Igbo, Hausa, or other Nigerian language terms
Important Note: All submissions will be reviewed for accuracy and relevance before inclusion. We will contact you to verify information and discuss proper attribution. Your contribution helps build a more complete and accurate historical record.

Submission Process

  1. Submit Information: Use the contact form to share your contribution
  2. Review & Verification: I will review submissions and may contact you for clarification
  3. Attribution: We will work with you to determine appropriate credit and citation
  4. Integration: Approved contributions will be added to the archive with acknowledgment
  5. Notification: You'll be notified when your contribution is published

Privacy & Permissions

By submitting information, you confirm that:

  • You have the right to share any images or documents provided
  • You consent to the inclusion of your contribution in this public archive
  • You understand that this is an academic project and contributions will not be compensated monetarily
  • You agree to the use of your name for attribution (or can request anonymity)

Partnership Opportunities

I am actively seeking partnerships with organizations committed to preserving and promoting Nigerian cultural heritage. Potential collaboration areas include:

  • Research Collaboration: Joint research projects and data sharing
  • Educational Programming: Workshops, lectures, and teaching resources
  • Exhibition Development: Physical or digital exhibitions based on project data
  • Community Workshops: Skill-sharing sessions with contemporary artisans
  • Resource Development: Creating accessible educational materials

Organizations of particular interest include Nigerian universities, cultural museums, textile societies, and heritage preservation initiatives.
