Community Spotlight

Celebrating Living Traditions & Contemporary Voices

Where history meets the present: Community members share their connections to Nigerian textile traditions

Connecting Past & Present

While the historical archive documents textile traditions from 1850-1960, these practices continue to thrive today through the hands and voices of contemporary artisans, cultural practitioners, and community members.

This Community Spotlight celebrates the living legacy of Nigerian women's textile traditions. Here, you'll find images and stories shared by community members wearing traditional dress, practicing ancestral crafts, or preserving cultural knowledge passed down through generations.

These contributions serve as contemporary ethnographic documentation, creating a richer, more complete understanding of how textile traditions have evolved, persisted, and adapted over time.

"My grandmother taught me adire dyeing when I was twelve. Every pattern tells a story—this one represents the journey of water, always flowing, always changing, but never losing its essence. When I dye cloth, I feel connected to all the women in my family who came before me."
Folake Adeyemi Lagos, Nigeria • Adire Dyeing
Contemporary Practice
"In Akwete, we say the loom is our library. Each pattern is a book, each thread is a word. My mother's hands taught mine, her mother's hands taught hers. This cloth carries five generations of women's wisdom."
Chioma Nwankwo Akwete, Abia State • Traditional Weaving
Intergenerational Practice
"This is my grandmother's wedding aso oke from 1947. We still wear it for special ceremonies. The gold threads have dulled but the love woven into each stripe remains bright. It reminds us that our traditions are not museum pieces—they are living, breathing parts of who we are."
Titilayo Ogunleye Ibadan, Nigeria • Family Heirloom
Historical Preservation

Share Your Story

Do you practice traditional textile crafts? Wear cultural dress? Preserve family heirlooms?

We invite you to contribute to the Community Spotlight and help document the living legacy of Nigerian textile traditions.

Submit Your Story & Images

Submission Guidelines

What We're Looking For:
  • Images of you in traditional Nigerian dress or cultural attire
  • Photos of you practicing textile crafts (weaving, dyeing, embroidery, etc.)
  • Images of traditional tools, looms, or materials you use
  • Family heirlooms or textiles with personal significance
  • Contemporary adaptations of traditional techniques
What to Include:
  • Your Name: How you'd like to be credited
  • Location: Your city/town and state
  • Craft/Tradition: What textile tradition is shown
  • Your Story: 2-4 sentences about your connection to this tradition (50-150 words)
  • Image: High-quality photo (JPEG or PNG, at least 1200px wide)
  • Permission: Confirmation that you own the image and consent to publication
Cultural Sensitivity:
  • We respect sacred or ceremonial traditions—only share what is appropriate to make public
  • Family permission is required for images of minors
  • You may request anonymity while still sharing your image and story
  • Images of religious or sacred textiles should only be submitted if culturally appropriate
Review Process: All submissions are reviewed for appropriateness and cultural sensitivity before publication. I will contact you to verify details and discuss any questions. You maintain copyright to your images while granting Threading Histories permission to display them with attribution.
